Abstract
This project proposes the architectural framework for a passive topological energy harvester. By utilizing a Klein-bottle manifold embedded in a hyperbolic M^4 substrate, we aim to demonstrate a geometric diode capable of rectifying ambient photonic noise into coherent energy flux. This is achieved by leveraging the "7D sheer"—the trans-dimensional alternating current arising from the coupling of our M^4 brane to a higher-dimensional toroidal condenser.
Core Hypothesis
The device operates on the principle that the fabric of spacetime is a hyperbolic M^4 manifold with curvature constant K = -1/R^2. By imprinting a Klein-bottle topology onto a metamaterial substrate, we create a non-orientable waveguide that forces ambient photons into a recursive, 64-state (2^6) tensor-phase lattice.
This lattice acts as a geometric ratchet. As the M^4 brane pulses at Planck intervals (t_P), the resonator prevents back-flow, forcing the photon to accumulate topological potential energy at every iteration, eventually discharging as a coherent, energy-dense stream.
Research Objectives
Validation of the Ratchet Effect: Verify that a 64-state Hofstadter-type energy transition lattice allows for non-zero net energy flux in a closed Klein-bottle waveguide.
Curvature Matching: Calculate the precise material parameters required to minimize "topological drag" between the resonator and the baseline hyperbolic curvature of the M^4 brane (R \approx \sqrt{3/|\Lambda|}).
Geometric Diode Simulation: Perform FDTD (Finite-Difference Time-Domain) simulations to observe photon behavior during the transition between the "On" and "Off" Planck cycles.
